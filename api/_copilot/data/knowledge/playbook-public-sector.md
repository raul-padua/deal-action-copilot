# Industry Playbook: Public Sector & Government

## Buying context
Agencies must stop benefits and program fraud while preserving access for legitimate
citizens — including thin-file, elderly, unbanked, and new-to-country populations. Equity
and accessibility requirements are usually written into the mandate, and procurement runs
through pilots, security assessments, and legislative oversight.

## Recommended motion
- Lead with the dual outcome: fraud reduction AND higher pass rates for hard-to-verify
  citizens. Never lead with fraud alone — access is the political requirement.
- Propose a scoped pilot with agreed success metrics before full rollout; agencies almost
  never buy big-bang.
- Address accessibility head-on: verification paths that do not require a smartphone or a
  driver's license (database verification first, document capture as one option among
  several, not a gate).
- Expect and welcome the security assessment; offer compliance documentation proactively.

## Approved claims
- Socure markets an AI-driven platform that helps agencies combat identity fraud without
  compromising access for the public.
- High auto-verify rates for thin-file and hard-to-identify populations reduce manual
  exception queues that disproportionately burden vulnerable citizens.

## Policy notes
- Do not name competing government identity providers in customer-facing materials.
- Pilot proposals and pricing structures require deal-desk involvement.
