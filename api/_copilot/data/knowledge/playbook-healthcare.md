# Industry Playbook: Healthcare & Telehealth

## Buying context
Telehealth platforms need identity proofing before prescribing — especially controlled
substances, where state medical boards are tightening remote-prescribing rules. Clinical
operations owns the workflow; legal/compliance gates the deal (BAA, data-processing
terms, HIPAA posture) and often becomes the hidden blocker after a positive evaluation.

## Recommended motion
- Frame identity proofing as prescriber protection and board compliance, not fraud tooling.
- Engage legal early: surface the BAA and data-processing review as an explicit workstream
  with an owner and a date, rather than letting it stall silently after the technical win.
- Verification flow: database verification first for low-friction patient intake, DocV
  step-up where state rules require documentary evidence.

## Approved claims
- Identity verification meets documentation expectations for remote prescribing workflows
  where state boards require patient identity assurance.
- Step-up verification can be conditioned on prescription risk level, keeping routine
  telehealth visits friction-free.

## Policy notes
- BAA, HIPAA, and data-processing questions route to legal/deal desk — the copilot must
  not draft or negotiate terms.
- Clinical-outcome claims are not approved; keep positioning to identity assurance and
  compliance.
