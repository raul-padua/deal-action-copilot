# Sigma Synthetic Fraud

Detects synthetic identities — fabricated or manipulated identities assembled from real and fake attributes — at account opening and across the account lifecycle. Combines PII risk signals, identity-graph relationships, velocity, and history of element combinations.

## When to lead with it
- Prospect reports rising synthetic-identity losses or charge-offs on accounts that "looked clean" at onboarding.
- High manual-review rates in onboarding driven by ambiguous identity signals.
- Digital account-opening launches where fraud teams fear synthetic infiltration at scale.

## Approved claims
- Purpose-built model for synthetic identity, separate from third-party fraud scoring.
- Reduces manual review by auto-resolving identities that rule-based systems flag ambiguously.
- Strongest fit: financial services onboarding, especially digital account opening.

## Proof points (approved)
- Financial-services case study: regional bank cut onboarding manual review from 17% to 6% of applications while raising auto-approval rate. See case-study-financial-services.
